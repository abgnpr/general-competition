# RBI Grade B — Syllabus & Exam Pattern

Source: [anujjindal.in — RBI Grade B Syllabus & Exam Pattern](https://anujjindal.in/rbi-grade-b-syllabus-exam-pattern/)

> The RBI official syllabus is only indicative, not defined. Phase 1 and Phase 2 share topical overlap (GA/ESI); management portion is largely static.

Selection stages: **Phase 1 (Prelims, objective)** → **Phase 2 (Mains, mostly descriptive)** → **Interview**.

---

## Phase 1 — Prelims (Objective)

- Single paper, **200 marks**, **120 minutes**.
- Sections: 4 (General Awareness, Quantitative Aptitude, Reasoning Ability, English Language).
- Questions: 320 total (80 per section).
- Each section holds equal importance.

### Section 1 — General Awareness (80 Q, 80 M)

#### Current Affairs

- National: Union Budget, government schemes, appointments, awards, defence news, books/authors.
- International: global developments, financial assistance, agreements, summits.
- Banking: sector news, RBI updates, economy-related developments.

#### Banking Awareness

- Indian banking system structure, bank types, financial institutions, money/banking concepts, digital payments.

#### Banking & RBI Specific Topics

- RBI functions, monetary policy, relevant acts/laws.

#### Static General Awareness

- Banking terminology, institutional awareness, reports/indexes, committees.

### Section 2 — Quantitative Aptitude (80 Q, 80 M)

#### Arithmetic

- Percentages, Ratio & Proportion, Profit/Loss & Discounts, Simple & Compound Interest, Ages & Partnerships, Average & Alligation, Mixtures, Time & Work, Pipes & Cisterns, Time/Speed/Distance, Trains & Boats.

#### Algebra, Number System & Mensuration

- Simplification, number system, quadratic equations, 2D/3D mensuration.

#### Data Interpretation & Advanced Math

- Bar/pie/line graphs, caselets, probability, permutation/combination.

### Section 3 — Reasoning Ability (80 Q, 80 M)

#### Logical Reasoning

- Syllogism, Inequalities (Direct & Coded), Statement & Conclusion, Statement & Assumption, Statement & Argument, Cause & Effect, Course of Action.

#### Analytical & Puzzle-Based Reasoning

- Floor/box/scheduling puzzles, seating arrangements, input-output, data sufficiency.

#### Basic Reasoning Concepts

- Coding-decoding, series, blood relations, direction sense, order/ranking.

### Section 4 — English Language (80 Q, 80 M)

#### Question Types

- Reading Comprehension, Gap Fillers, Double Fillers, Error Spotting, Sentence Rearrangement, Idiom/Phrases Usage & Replacement, Word Reshuffle/Swapping, Para Jumbles, Sentence Connectors, Odd One Out, Sentence Completion, Sentence Correction, Word Replacement, Cloze Test.

#### Grammar

- Subject-verb agreement, parts of speech, tenses, articles, conditionals, modals, voice.

---

## Phase 2 — Mains

- Three papers, **100 marks each**.
- Duration: **120 + 120 + 90 minutes** (ESI, Finance & Management, English Descriptive respectively).
- Subjects: Economic & Social Issues (ESI), Finance, Management, English.
- Sectional cut-offs apply in addition to the overall cut-off.

### Paper I — Economic & Social Issues (ESI) — Descriptive

#### 1. Basic Economic Concepts

- National income accounting, schools of economic thought, growth/development, human development, sustainable development.

#### 2. Indian Economic Development

- Pre-1947 overview.
- Post-1947 through 1991: macro, agriculture, industry, services, external sector.
- Post-1991: same subsectors.

#### 3. Public Finance & Macroeconomic Policies

- Public Expenditure and Receipts, Public Debt, Fiscal Policy, Federal Finance, Inflation, Indian Taxation System, Planning Period in India.

#### 4. Social & Development Issues

- Poverty, Unemployment, Population Growth and Demographic Issues, Health, Education, Gender, Positive Discrimination, Multiculturalism, Urbanisation and Migration.

#### 5. Banking, Institutions & Development

- International economic institutions, banking/RBI role, rural credit, microfinance.

### Paper II — English Descriptive (Writing Skills) — Descriptive

#### 1. Essay Writing

- Topics from economic/financial issues, social developments, governance, technology, environment, ethics.

#### 2. Precis Writing

- Long passage condensed into a precis of about 170–180 words.

#### 3. Reading Comprehension (Descriptive)

- Passage-based questions requiring short structured answers.

### Paper III — Finance & Management — Descriptive (combined)

#### Finance — 1. Banking & Financial System

- Regulatory Bodies in Financial Sector, Indian Banking System, Non-Banking Systems, Indian Financial System, Financial Markets, Risk Management in Banking Sector, Corporate Governance in Banking Sector, Financial Inclusion.

#### Finance — 2. Global Finance

- FinTech, global markets/banking, financial crisis impact, digital payments.

#### Finance — 3. Public Finance & Government Policies

- Inflation, FDI, FPI and Disinvestment, GST, Indian Taxation System and Public Expenditure, FRBM Act and Finance Commission, Alternate Sources of Finance, PPP and Social Cost–Benefit.

#### Finance — 4. Accounting & Financial Analysis

- Basics, final accounts preparation, cash flow statements, ratio analysis.

#### Management — 1. Principles of Management

- Introduction, evolution of thought, management functions, organization structure.

#### Management — 2. Organisational Behaviour

- Introduction to Organisational Behaviour, Personality, Perception, Motivation, Stress and Learning, Analysis of Interpersonal Relationship.

#### Management — 3. Leadership & Managerial Skills

- Leadership, Authority and Power, Communication, Emotional Intelligence, Decision Making and MBO, Organisational Conflict.

#### Management — 4. Organisational Development & Ethics

- Organisational Change and Group, Organisational Development, Nudge Theory, Ethics, Corporate Governance.

---

## Phase 3 — Interview

- **75 marks**, **20–30 minutes**.
- No fixed syllabus.
- Assessment areas: personal background, RBI functions/mechanisms, Indian/global economic trends, government schemes, current affairs, situation-based questions, decision-making.
- Evaluation criteria: Analytical Decision Making, Clarity of Thought and Expression, Mental Alertness, Depth of Knowledge, Thought Leadership, Communication Skills, Behaviour and Attitude, Understanding of Indian Economic Policies, Current Affairs and General Awareness, Global Economic Trends and Impact on India.

---

## Special Streams

### DEPR (Department of Economic and Policy Research)

- Economics papers at Master's degree level + English (writing skills).

### DSIM (Department of Statistics and Information Management)

- Paper 1 (Objective): probability, sampling, ANOVA, hypothesis testing, multivariate analysis, stochastic processes.
- Paper 2 (Descriptive): statistical inference, linear models, sampling, stochastic processes, multivariate analysis, numerical analysis.
- Paper 3: English (writing skills).

|------|---------------:|---------------:|--------------:|
| 2025 | 77.5           | —              | —             |
| 2024 | 67.25          | 173.50         | 226.75        |
| 2023 | 54.25          | 169            | 229           |
| 2022 | 63.75          | 171.25         | 234.50        |

Stability pattern:

- Phase 1 cutoff: **unstable**, swings roughly **30–40%** year-on-year.
- Phase 2 cutoff: **stable**, around **~57%**.
- Final cutoff: **stable**, around **~62%**.

Takeaway: target Phase 2 / Final at the stable band; treat Phase 1 as a volatile gate — aim well above recent highs rather than averages.
