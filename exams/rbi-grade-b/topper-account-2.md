# RBI Grade B DR 2019 — Topper Strategy (Amit Punia)

## Exam Structure Overview

| Phase | Sections | Marks |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Phase 1 | GA (80) + Reasoning (60) + English (30) + Quant (30) | 200 |
| Phase 2 | ESI + FM + Descriptive | — |
| Interview | — | — |

---

## Phase 1

**Score: 147.5** (GA 67.5 + Quant 29.75 + English 28.75 + Reasoning 21.5)

Phase 1 is just an elimination round and hence is the toughest. The key to clearing it comfortably is **GA and Reasoning**. Target at least **130 marks** (60 GA + 30 Reasoning + 20 each in Quant and English).

### GA

- **Sources:** Affairscloud + newspaper + PIB. Monthly GA quizzes on Gradeup were very helpful.
- **Notes:** Made GA notes on a daily basis, updated regularly from monthly Affairscloud PDFs and mock tests. Preferred **digital notes** for editing flexibility. Prepared notes keeping both Phase 1 and Phase 2 in mind.
- 4-6 months GA is sufficient, along with Budget and Economic Survey.
- **Revision is the key to scoring high in GA.**

### Reasoning

- Followed Adda247 daily quizzes — very useful from exam point of view.
- With sectional cutoff, preferred attempting non-puzzle questions first: **inequalities, blood relations, direction-based questions, assertion-reason type questions** to clear sectional cutoff first.
- Attempted puzzles only in remaining time.

### Quant

- No specific preparation — just attempted daily quizzes on Adda247 before Phase 1.

### English

- No specific preparation — questions were very basic in nature.

### Phase 1 Tips

1. Go through memory-based past papers to understand the scope of questions.
2. Attempt as many mocks as you can — try multiple platforms (1-2 are generally free).
3. Don't start with puzzles in Reasoning — clear sectional cutoff first.
4. Attempt as many questions as you can — smart guesswork benefits when negative marking is just 0.25.
5. After Phase 1, don't waste time on cutoff/result date speculation. Start revising Phase 2 notes immediately.

## Phase 2

Had already covered Phase 2 syllabus (static topics) before the notification was published. Utilized the time between Phase 1 and 2 **only for revision** (multiple times).

**Target: at least 220-230 in Phase 2** (interview stage is very unpredictable).

### ESI

| Area | Sources & Approach |
|------|-------------------|
| Static topics (Growth, Poverty, Environment, Reforms) | Summary sheets from Telegram groups |
| Old Govt schemes | PDF from Skidha's blog, converted to Word, updated from operating guidelines on ministry websites |
| New Govt schemes | Covered alongside GA preparation |
| Dynamic topics | PIB, newspapers (Indian Express, LiveMint), Affairscloud |
| Social issues | Health, Education, Women, Tribals, Social Justice — covered in detail |
| Budget, Eco Survey, SECC | In-depth coverage |
| Reports & indices | UN agencies, Govt committees, NITI Aayog, multilateral organizations — categorized in notes |

- **Memorize the entire syllabus** to filter irrelevant factual GA while making notes.
- Cover PIB yourself — don't rely on compilations. PIB is very rich for ESI, especially when Parliament is in session. Focus on: Govt schemes, projects, initiatives, campaigns, summits, Govt targets, cabinet decisions.
- **Target: at least 70 in ESI.**

### Finance & Management

**Finance:**

- Static topics: free summary sheets.
- Dynamic topics: PIB, newspapers, Affairscloud, RBI Website, committee reports & working group reports.
- Secondary source: **Investopedia** for better understanding.
- Focused on news related to: BIS, OECD, FATF, IMF, G20, WB, ILO, AIIB, NDB, ISA.
- Don't skip numericals — RBI may surprise you even if they haven't asked them recently.

**Management:**

- Made topic-wise handwritten notes compiled from multiple sources.
- Sources: *Principles of Management* by PC Tripathi, free summary sheets, managementstudyguide.com.
- **Focus on identifying "keywords"** related to various theories and models — helps answer confusing questions (e.g., career path vs career ladder, job description vs job specification, leadership theories).

**Target: at least 85+ in FM.**

### Descriptive

- Prepared 5-6 essay topics; collected fodder points for 10-12 potential topics.
- Used internet for precis and letter writing formats.
- Used factual data from GA preparation to enrich essays.

### Phase 2 Tips

1. Complete Phase 2 syllabus **before notification is released**.
2. Revise the entire syllabus at least 2-3 times.
3. Make a timetable and stick to it.
4. Go through previous year papers to understand scope.
5. For management, focus on keywords to identify theories and models.
6. Don't neglect the Descriptive paper.
7. Attempt as many questions as possible — smart guesses benefit with 0.25 negative marking.
8. Don't waste time on Telegram/WhatsApp groups — go only to collect exam-relevant material.
9. **Exam can be cracked without buying any coaching course.**
10. Telegram groups for MCQ practice: RBI Grade B Questionnaire, RBI Grade B MCQs, MCQs on Schemes/Reports/PIB/Budget.

## Interview

Had almost 3 months to prepare. Took a holistic approach:

### Preparation

- Wrote down hundreds of potential questions based on: profile, previous job (SBI), recent events, RBI functions, banking and finance.
- **Must read:** *Functions and Working of RBI* published by RBSC.
- Went through: RBI Website, Annual Report, Monetary Policy Report, Financial Stability Report, latest speeches, committee reports — made notes from all.
- Previous year transcripts helped plug gaps in preparation.
- Attended 1 free mock interview (Edutap).

### Interview Tips

1. **Never bluff.** Be humble and honest. It's okay to say "Sorry, I don't know."
2. Have a good justification for your education and career choices — grilling happens when you can't defend your position.
3. Be ready to give a 2-3 minute speech on any topic given by the RBISB chairman.
4. Be calm and composed. Avoid unnecessary hand/leg movement. Keep hands free from items like ID cards, pens.
5. **Be crisp in answers.** The board looks for specific keywords. They prefer bookish definitions — converse like an economist, not a common man.
6. Smile but don't laugh.
7. Maintain a positive outlook in answers.
8. **Believe in yourself.** Don't go with preconceived notions about your profile — the board is experienced and every candidate has an equal chance.

## Key Takeaways

1. **GA and Reasoning** are the keys to Phase 1.
2. **Digital notes** with daily updates give flexibility and thoroughness.
3. **Complete Phase 2 syllabus before notification** — use the gap only for revision.
4. **Keywords** in Management help crack confusing questions.
5. **Cover PIB first-hand** — it's the richest source for ESI.
6. **Attempt maximum questions** with smart guessing at 0.25 negative marking.
7. **No coaching needed** — free resources and self-study suffice.
8. For interview, prepare exhaustively from RBI's own publications and reports.
