# RBI Grade B 2018 — Topper Strategy (AIR 67)

## Marks Breakdown

| Stage | Marks |
|-------|-------|
| Prelims | 112.5 |
| Mains | 218.5 |
| Interview | 30 |
| **Total** | **248.5** |

## Background

- B.Tech (EXTC) from VJTI Mumbai, graduated 2015.
- Was preparing for UPSC Civil Services since 2015; had given 2 mains before RBI.
- Decent in Quant and Reasoning, good in English, **zero knowledge of Finance & Management**.
- Started serious RBI prep only after the notification was out.

---

## Prelims Strategy

### Time Management (200 questions in 2 hours)

| Section | Time | Order |
|---------|------|-------|
| GA | 15-20 min | 1st |
| English | 20-25 min | 2nd |
| Quant | 30 min | 3rd |
| Reasoning | 45 min | 4th |

Attempted all questions in English, 79 in GA, 14 in Quant, and 26 in Reasoning. Many people take Reasoning first and then find themselves with little time for Quant, failing to clear the sectional cutoff. *(Note: sectional timing has since been introduced, easing this.)*

### Quant (30 questions)

- ~45 days between notification and prelims — jotted down syllabus, identified easy areas.
- **Targeted:** Number Series and DI (Diagram Interpretation) — these alone account for 10-15 questions, enough to clear cutoff.
- Relied on YouTube (Amar Sir's videos). Select a topic, watch all conceptual videos, then apply in mocks.

### Reasoning

- **Targeted:** Syllogisms, Inequalities, Directions, Coded Inequalities, Data Sufficiency.
- **Left puzzles entirely** (30 marks). Solving puzzles can consume time and a small mistake costs dearly. Complete easy questions first, then attempt puzzles if time permits.

### English

- No dedicated preparation — aimed to solve all questions as fast as possible.
- Was scoring 25+ in mocks already. Those struggling should watch targeted videos on YouTube by searching syllabus topics.

### GA Sources

| Source | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| GKToday Monthly (one-liner summary) | Current affairs |
| BankersAdda Capsule for RBI | Banking-specific GK |
| Vision PT365 — Economy, Social Issues, Govt Schemes | Static + current |
| IAS Baba Summary of Budget | Budget prep |
| Vision Summary of Economic Survey | Economic survey |
| Oliveboard PDF + Wikipedia | National parks & sanctuaries |
| Sriram's IAS Economy | Economy concepts |
| One business newspaper (LiveMint) | Daily reading |

- 4 months of current affairs suffice; focus more on the 2 months immediately before the exam.
- Keep material limited, revise regularly.
- Made personal notes in a dedicated RBI notebook — budget, growth forecasts by institutions, fund allocations by WB/IMF, committees/reports (chairman & cause).

## Mains Strategy

Had ~25 days after prelims (shortened due to Kerala floods). 50% of time went to Finance & Management.

### Finance & Management (FM)

- **Primary source:** Manish Kumar Sir's YouTube channel — *best material available*.
- **Secondary:** Edutap notes (market-available).
- Prepared own notes for last-minute revision.
- Focus on understanding concepts; formulae follow automatically.
- In the exam, for unfamiliar questions, eliminate wrong options and apply existing knowledge.
- **Attempt all questions.**

### Economic & Social Issues (ESI)

- Revised GA material prepared for prelims, plus current affairs from the 25 new days after prelims.
- Devote extra time to government schemes — many questions are based on them.
- For trivial questions about old schemes, use elimination to narrow down answers.
- **Attempt all questions.**

### Descriptive English — Essays

- **Source:** Edutap releases 20-25 essay topics with fodder material. Prepare at least the top 10 hot topics.
- Only 1 out of 4 essays needs to be written — high chance of getting a prepared topic.
- **Essay structure (4 paragraphs):**
  1. Introduction
  2. Positives / Opportunities
  3. Negatives / Challenges
  4. Conclusion
- Incorporate data, facts, quotes, or committee recommendations.
- Also includes precis and passage components.

### Word Limit Discipline

- Stick as close to the word limit as possible (e.g., 298/300 words).
- Avoid typos and grammatical mistakes — they directly reduce marks.

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Keep the approach simple.** The exam is simple; we tend to complicate it.
2. **Rely on limited material** and revise it religiously.
3. **Make your own notes** — the act of writing aids retention and last-minute revision.
4. **Understand concepts** rather than memorizing formulae.
5. **Attempt maximum questions** — especially in ESI and FM.
6. **Interview is the easiest stage** with ample preparation time.
