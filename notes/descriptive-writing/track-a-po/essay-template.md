# Essay Template

Target length: ~250 words. Time: ~15–20 minutes. 4-paragraph structure.

## Structure

```text
[Paragraph 1 — Introduction, ~50 words]
- Define / frame the topic in 1 sentence.
- Why it matters today (recent context, stat, event).
- Thesis: what the essay will argue / cover.

[Paragraph 2 — Current state / problem, ~70 words]
- What is the situation today?
- 1–2 data points or recent examples.
- What's driving it?

[Paragraph 3 — Analysis / solutions / counter-view, ~80 words]
- Causes, implications, or solutions (pick the frame the topic invites).
- Government schemes / initiatives (named).
- One counter-view or limitation (shows balance).

[Paragraph 4 — Conclusion, ~50 words]
- Synthesize — don't repeat.
- Forward-looking: what needs to happen.
- One closing line that lands.
```

## Topic-frame decision tree

| Topic invites | Paragraph 3 frame |
|---------------|-------------------|
| "Discuss the impact of X" | Effects (positive + negative) |
| "How to address X" | Solutions / policy recommendations |
| "Pros and cons of X" | Balanced argument |
| "Role of X in Y" | Functional breakdown |

## Reusable intros (adapt the blank)

- *"In an era defined by [______], the question of [topic] has acquired renewed urgency."*
- *"The intersection of [A] and [B] has become one of the defining challenges of our time."*
- *"India's experience with [topic] reflects both remarkable progress and persistent gaps."*

## Reusable transitions

- *"However, this progress has not been uniform…"*
- *"On the other hand…"*
- *"A closer examination reveals…"*
- *"That said, critics argue…"*

## Reusable conclusions

- *"The way forward lies in [specific action], supported by [stakeholder/policy]."*
- *"Ultimately, [topic] is less a problem to be solved than a continuous process to be managed."*
- *"As India moves toward [goal], [topic] will remain a central test of its ambitions."*

## The "insert-data" rule

Every essay needs at least **one specific data point** — a year, a percentage, a scheme name, a report. Generic essays score average. One concrete number lifts you above the median.

Keep a running stockpile in [reusable-paras.md](reusable-paras.md).

## Do / Don't

**Do:**

- One idea per paragraph.
- Short sentences. 15-word average.
- Name schemes/reports — Jan Dhan, PMJDY, Ayushman Bharat, Niti Aayog, RBI Bulletin.

**Don't:**

- Stuff all 5 views into one paragraph.
- Vague claims ("many people believe…"). Be specific.
- Rhetorical questions. Examiners read them as filler.
- Poetic openings ("Since the dawn of time…"). Get to the point.
