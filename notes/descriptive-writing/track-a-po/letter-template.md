# Letter Template

Target length: ~150 words. Time: ~10 minutes. Structure is fixed; only content varies.

## Formal Letter Structure

```text
[Sender's address]
[Date]

[Recipient's designation]
[Recipient's address]

Subject: <one-line, specific>

Dear Sir / Madam,

[Paragraph 1 — context and purpose]
State who you are (if needed) and the reason for writing. 2–3 sentences.

[Paragraph 2 — details]
Expand the issue: what happened, when, any reference numbers. 3–4 sentences.

[Paragraph 3 — request / expected action]
Ask clearly for the action you want. State timeline if relevant. 2 sentences.

Thank you for your attention to this matter.

Yours faithfully,
[Name]
```

## Informal Letter Structure

```text
[Sender's address]
[Date]

Dear <Name>,

[Paragraph 1 — opening warmth + reason for writing]

[Paragraph 2 — the actual content: advice / congratulations / shared experience]

[Paragraph 3 — closing, forward look, hope to hear back]

Yours lovingly / Your friend,
[Name]
```

## Category checklist (what to prep)

**Formal:**

- Complaint to bank manager — service / account / product.
- Request to employer — leave / transfer / loan.
- Letter to editor — social issue / civic concern.
- Letter to authority — suggestion / grievance.

**Informal:**

- Advice to sibling / friend — career / life decision.
- Congratulations — achievement / promotion.
- Shared experience — travel / event.

## Do / Don't

**Do:**

- Keep sentences short (12–18 words).
- Use specific details (date, amount, reference).
- Polite but direct tone.

**Don't:**

- Fancy vocabulary. Simple > impressive.
- Long introductions. Get to the point by sentence 2.
- Informal contractions ("don't", "can't") in formal letters.

## Reusable openings

- *"I am writing to bring to your attention…"*
- *"I wish to lodge a formal complaint regarding…"*
- *"I am writing in reference to…"*

## Reusable closings

- *"I trust you will look into this matter at the earliest."*
- *"I would appreciate a response within [timeframe]."*
- *"Thank you for your prompt attention to this issue."*
