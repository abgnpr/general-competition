# Grammar Basics

**Tier 1 · Priority 3.** The 5 rules that decide 90% of Error Spotting / Sentence Improvement.

Status: not started

## The Big 5 (Pareto)

1. **Subject-Verb Agreement** — singular subject → singular verb. Trickier with collective nouns, "each/every", "either/neither", "and"/"or" subjects.
2. **Tenses** — past vs present perfect, past perfect sequencing, conditionals.
3. **Prepositions** — fixed phrases (depend on, agree with, different from), verb-preposition combos.
4. **Articles** — a/an/the. Zero article before proper nouns, uncountables without a/an, specific vs general.
5. **Pronouns** — antecedent agreement, case (I/me, who/whom), reflexive vs reciprocal.

## Tier 2 Grammar (after Big 5)

- Modifiers (dangling, misplaced)
- Parallelism (lists, comparisons)
- Comparison degree (better/best, fewer/less)
- Conjunctions (not only...but also, either...or)
- Conditionals (zero, first, second, third)

## Subject-Verb Agreement — traps

- *"Each of the students is/are..."* → **is** (each = singular).
- *"The committee is/are divided"* → context-dependent (US = is, UK = are).
- *"Neither A nor B is/are"* → verb agrees with nearer subject.
- *"Data is/are"* → technically plural, often treated as singular.

## Tense — traps

- Past perfect used only with two past actions (the earlier one is perfect).
- "Since" requires present perfect, not simple past.
- Conditional type III: *If I had known, I would have gone* (not *would have knew*).

## Articles — traps

- No article before proper nouns (India, not "the India") — *but* "the United States", "the Netherlands".
- "A university" (you-niversity — consonant sound), not "an university".
- "The" before superlatives, unique entities, musical instruments (the piano).

## Pronoun — traps

- *"Between you and I/me"* → **me** (object of preposition).
- *"Whom did you see?"* → **whom** (object).
- *"Me and him went"* → **He and I went**.
