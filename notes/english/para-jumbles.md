# Para Jumbles

**Tier 1 · Priority 5.** Logic + connector awareness.

Status: not started

## Approach

1. Identify the **opening sentence** — introduces the topic, has no back-reference (no "this", "however", "therefore" pointing backward).
2. Identify the **closing sentence** — often a conclusion, forward-look, or summary.
3. Chain the middle using **connectors** and **referent words** (this, those, such, he, it).
4. Check the chain reads coherently. Redo if the flow feels forced.

## Connector Cues (forward vs backward)

| Direction | Cues |
|-----------|------|
| Backward-looking (can't be first) | however, therefore, moreover, this, those, such, as a result |
| Forward-looking (can start a chain) | Topic-introducing statements, definitions, historical context |

## Order-Finding Tricks

- **Proper nouns before pronouns** — "Raj went to the market. He bought bread." — the sentence with "Raj" comes first.
- **General before specific** — topic sentence before examples.
- **Problem before solution** — framing before resolution.
- **Time order** — chronological clues (first, then, later, finally, today).

## Common Traps

- Two sentences that sound like they could both be the opener → check which one's referent would be floating.
- "However" / "But" tempting you to place mid-chain when it should be later.
- Pronoun-referent mismatch: if "he" appears, the named person must be in an earlier sentence.

## New-Pattern Variants

- **Odd-one-out** — one sentence doesn't belong. Identify the chain of 4, the 5th is out.
- **Fix-the-first-sentence** — first sentence given, rearrange the rest.
