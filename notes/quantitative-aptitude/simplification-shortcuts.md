## 1. VBODMAS Rule (Order of Operations)

This is the **golden rule** of simplification. Always solve expressions in this order:

| Letter | Stands For | Meaning |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **V** | Vinculum | The bar above a group of numbers (e.g., ̅ ) |
| **B** | Brackets | Solve innermost first: `( )` → `{ }` → `[ ]` |
| **O** | Of | Means "multiplication" (e.g., ½ of 40 = 20) |
| **D** | Division | ÷ |
| **M** | Multiplication | × |
| **A** | Addition | + |
| **S** | Subtraction | − |

> **Key Point:** Division and Multiplication are performed left to right (equal priority). Same for Addition and Subtraction.

## 4. Divisibility Rules

Quick checks to avoid unnecessary long division:

| Divisible by | Rule |
|-------------|------|
| **2** | Last digit is 0, 2, 4, 6, or 8 |
| **3** | Sum of all digits is divisible by 3 |
| **4** | Last two digits form a number divisible by 4 |
| **5** | Last digit is 0 or 5 |
| **6** | Divisible by both 2 and 3 |
| **7** | Double the last digit, subtract from remaining number; result divisible by 7 |
| **8** | Last three digits form a number divisible by 8 |
| **9** | Sum of all digits is divisible by 9 |
| **10** | Last digit is 0 |
| **11** | Difference of sum of digits at odd and even places is 0 or divisible by 11 |
| **12** | Divisible by both 3 and 4 |
| **15** | Divisible by both 3 and 5 |
| **25** | Last two digits are 00, 25, 50, or 75 |

## 5. Digital Sum Method (Check Your Answer Instantly)

The **digital sum** of a number is found by repeatedly adding its digits until you get a single digit.

**Example:** 293 → 2 + 9 + 3 = 14 → 1 + 4 = **5**

**Shortcut:** You can **omit 9s** while adding to save time.
- 293 → 2 + ~~9~~ + 3 = **5** (same answer, faster)

### How to use it:
If you're multiplying 326 × 890, compute the digital sums:
- DS(326) = 3 + 2 + 6 = 11 → 2
- DS(890) = 8 + 9 + 0 = 17 → 8
- DS(product) = 2 × 8 = 16 → 7

Now check which answer option has a digital sum of 7. This eliminates wrong options instantly.

> **Note:** If any number is multiplied by 9, the digital sum is always 9.

## 6. Approximation Technique

For questions that say **"approximate value"**, don't calculate exactly:

- Round numbers to the nearest tens/hundreds.
- Example: 150.4 × 5.876 ≈ 150 × 6 = **900**
- Example: 289 + 336 → (300 − 11) + (300 + 36) = **625**

### When to use:
- When options are spread far apart.
- When the question uses words like "approximately" or "≈".

## 8. Laws of Indices (Surds & Powers)

| Rule | Formula |
|------|---------|
| Multiplication | aᵐ × aⁿ = aᵐ⁺ⁿ |
| Division | aᵐ ÷ aⁿ = aᵐ⁻ⁿ |
| Power of a power | (aᵐ)ⁿ = aᵐⁿ |
| Zero power | a⁰ = 1 |
| Negative power | a⁻ⁿ = 1/aⁿ |
| Fractional power | a^(1/n) = ⁿ√a |

**Tip:** Convert all numbers to the same base before simplifying.

Example: 8¹² ÷ 16² of 32³ × √256
→ (2³)¹² ÷ (2⁴)² of (2⁵)³ × 2⁴
→ 2³⁶ ÷ 2⁸ of 2¹⁵ × 2⁴ → solve using index laws.

## 9. Fraction Arithmetic Shortcuts

### Adding/Subtracting fractions quickly:
- **a/b + c/d** = (ad + bc) / bd
- **a/b − c/d** = (ad − bc) / bd

### Comparing fractions:
- Cross multiply: If a/b vs c/d → compare a×d with b×c.

### Mixed to Improper:
- 3¼ = (3 × 4 + 1)/4 = 13/4

## 10. Decimal Shortcuts

### Multiplying decimals:
1. Ignore decimals, multiply whole numbers.
2. Count total decimal places in both numbers.
3. Place the decimal in the result.

**Example:** 1.2 × 3.4 → 12 × 34 = 408 → 2 decimal places → **4.08**

### Dividing decimals:
Move the decimal point right in both dividend and divisor until the divisor becomes whole, then divide normally.

## 12. Quick Multiplication Tricks

### Multiplying by 11:
- 23 × 11 → write digits with their sum in between: 2 (2+3) 3 = **253**
- If sum > 9, carry over: 87 × 11 → 8 (8+7=15) 7 → 8+1=9, so **957**

### Multiplying by 5:
- Multiply by 10, then divide by 2.
- 246 × 5 = 2460 ÷ 2 = **1230**

### Multiplying by 25:
- Multiply by 100, then divide by 4.
- 48 × 25 = 4800 ÷ 4 = **1200**

### Multiplying by 125:
- Multiply by 1000, then divide by 8.
- 64 × 125 = 64000 ÷ 8 = **8000**

### Squaring numbers ending in 5:
- n5² = n × (n+1) followed by 25.
- 35² = 3 × 4 = 12, append 25 → **1225**
- 85² = 8 × 9 = 72, append 25 → **7225**

## 13. Number Splitting Technique

Break complex numbers into simpler parts:

- 289 + 336 = (300 − 11) + (300 + 36) = 600 + 25 = **625**
- 47 × 99 = 47 × (100 − 1) = 4700 − 47 = **4653**
- 56 × 102 = 56 × (100 + 2) = 5600 + 112 = **5712**

## 14. Elimination Technique (Use Options!)

In MCQ-based exams, use the answer options to your advantage:
1. **Digit sum check:** Match the digital sum of your calculation with options.
2. **Last digit check:** Find only the unit digit of the answer to eliminate options.
3. **Even/Odd check:** Determine if the answer should be even or odd.
4. **Range estimation:** Roughly estimate and eliminate clearly wrong options.

## 15. Unit Digit Shortcuts

The unit digit of powers follows a cycle:

| Digit | Cycle | Period |
|-------|-------|--------|
| 0 | 0 | 1 |
| 1 | 1 | 1 |
| 2 | 2, 4, 8, 6 | 4 |
| 3 | 3, 9, 7, 1 | 4 |
| 4 | 4, 6 | 2 |
| 5 | 5 | 1 |
| 6 | 6 | 1 |
| 7 | 7, 9, 3, 1 | 4 |
| 8 | 8, 4, 2, 6 | 4 |
| 9 | 9, 1 | 2 |

**Example:** Unit digit of 7⁵³ → 53 ÷ 4 = remainder 1 → 1st in cycle → **7**

## 17. Key Formulas at a Glance

- **Sum of first n natural numbers:** n(n+1)/2
- **Sum of squares of first n numbers:** n(n+1)(2n+1)/6
- **Sum of cubes of first n numbers:** [n(n+1)/2]²
- **Product of two numbers** = HCF × LCM
- **Difference of squares:** a² − b² = (a+b)(a−b)
