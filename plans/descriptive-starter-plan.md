# Descriptive Writing — Starter Plan (Two Tracks)

Descriptive prep is **two completely different games**. Don't mix them.

| Track | Exams | Skill | Time to competence |
|-------|-------|-------|---------------------|
| **A. PO-style letter + essay** | SBI PO Mains, IBPS PO Mains, LIC AAO Mains | Format + templates + current-affairs topicals | 2–3 weeks |
| **B. Theme-based policy writing** | RBI Grade B Phase 2, NABARD Phase 2, SEBI Phase 2 | Subject depth (Econ/Finance/Mgmt/ARD) + structured answers + data/examples | 10–12 weeks, parallel with objective |

**Priority logic:** Track A is low-effort, high-don't-bomb-it insurance for PO exams. Track B is the actual rank-decider for RBI/NABARD/SEBI. If RBI/NABARD/SEBI are your serious targets, start Track B **now, in parallel** with quant. If PO is the first exam on your calendar, prep Track A in its final 3 weeks.

---

## Track A — PO Letter + Essay (SBI PO / IBPS PO / LIC AAO)

### Structure of the exam

- Usually 50 marks (SBI PO) or 25 marks (IBPS PO, LIC AAO).
- 1 letter (≈150 words) + 1 essay (≈250 words).
- Time: 30 minutes.
- Evaluated on: structure, grammar, relevance, length. **Not** on originality.

### Pareto — what actually moves the needle

- **Format mastery** (40%): formal/informal letter, essay intro/body/conclusion. Templates carry you.
- **Grammar + sentence clarity** (30%): no silly grammar errors. Short sentences beat complex.
- **Topic coverage** (20%): ~10 essay categories cover 80% of past papers. Prep 2–3 essays per category.
- **Handwriting / typing speed** (10%): depends on mode. Practice in exam mode.

### The 10 essay categories (past-paper Pareto)

1. Digital banking / cashless economy / fintech
2. Financial inclusion / Jan Dhan / priority sector
3. Women empowerment / gender gap / workforce
4. Climate change / green finance / sustainability
5. AI / technology in banking / cybersecurity
6. Education / skill development / youth unemployment
7. Healthcare / public health / Ayushman Bharat
8. Agriculture / farmer welfare / MSP / rural credit
9. Social media / mental health / misinformation
10. Economy / GDP / inflation / MSMEs

### Letter categories (past-paper Pareto)

- Formal: complaint (bank service, product), request (leave, loan, account), suggestion (to editor, to authority).
- Informal: advice, congratulations, shared experience.

### 3-week plan

**Week 1 — Templates**

- Day 1–2: Memorize one letter template (formal) + one essay template (4-para structure).
- Day 3–5: Write one letter and one essay per day. Me review structure and flag grammar.
- Day 6–7: Redraft the worst two.

**Week 2 — Topical coverage**

- Rotate through the 10 categories. 2 per day, write 1 essay each.
- Build a "reusable paragraphs" file (`notes/descriptive-writing/reusable-paras.md`): intros, conclusions, transitions, data points you can drop into any essay.

**Week 3 — Timed exam mode**

- Full 30-min drills. Letter + essay under pressure.
- Me score on structure, grammar, relevance. Iterate.

## Track B — RBI / NABARD / SEBI Descriptive (theme-based)

### Structure of the exam (high level)

- **RBI Phase 2:** Paper 1 (ESI — Economic & Social Issues, descriptive), Paper 2 (English descriptive — essay/precis/RC), Paper 3 (F&M — Finance & Management, descriptive).
- **NABARD Phase 2:** Paper 2 (ESI + ARD — Agriculture & Rural Development, descriptive).
- **SEBI Phase 2:** Paper 2 (English descriptive).
- Duration ~90 min per paper. Answer length 300–600 words per question.

### Pareto — what actually moves the needle

- **Subject depth** (50%): you are being tested on *knowledge*, not writing. Weak content = low marks regardless of prose.
- **Answer structure** (25%): intro → issue → data/examples → counter-view → conclusion + recommendation. Evaluators scan for this skeleton.
- **Data + examples** (15%): 1 stat per para. Names of schemes, committees, recent reports. This is the differentiator between average and top answers.
- **Clarity of prose** (10%): short sentences, active voice, no jargon padding.

### The theme-first approach (the cheat code)

Do NOT prep "essay topics." Prep **themes**. One theme covers 5–10 different essay/short-answer prompts. Example: the theme "Monetary Policy Transmission" can answer questions on repo rate, inflation targeting, MPC, liquidity, bank lending, real rates, etc.

### Theme library (starter — ~20 themes cover 80% of Phase 2)

**Economic & Social Issues (RBI / NABARD):**

1. Monetary policy, inflation, MPC
2. Fiscal policy, deficit, FRBM
3. GDP, growth, sectoral composition
4. Financial inclusion, priority sector, PSL
5. Banking sector reforms, NPAs, PCA
6. Poverty, inequality, human development index
7. Agriculture, MSP, APMC, rural credit, KCC
8. Unemployment, informal economy, labour reforms
9. Climate change, green finance, sustainable development
10. External sector, BoP, forex, trade deficit

**Finance & Management (RBI):**

11. Capital markets, SEBI, primary/secondary market
12. Risk management (credit, market, operational, Basel)
13. Corporate governance, board structure
14. Leadership and motivation theories (Maslow, Herzberg, McGregor)
15. Change management, organizational behaviour

**ARD (NABARD specific):**

16. Cropping patterns, green revolution, MSP, e-NAM
17. Rural credit structure, NABARD's role, SHG-BLP
18. Watershed development, climate-resilient agriculture
19. Allied sectors (dairy, fisheries, poultry, sericulture)
20. Rural infrastructure (RIDF, PMGSY, BharatNet)

### Theme-note format (mandatory structure)

Every theme note is 1 page max, in [notes/descriptive-writing/themes/](../notes/descriptive-writing/themes/):

- **Definition / core concept** — 2 sentences.
- **Key data points** — 3–5 latest stats with year/source.
- **Schemes / committees / reports** — named references (RBI report X, Niti Aayog report Y).
- **Current affairs linkage** — 2 recent news items (last 6 months) connecting to the theme.
- **Counter-view / limitations** — examiners love balance.
- **1-line conclusion hook** — a reusable closing sentence.

### 12-week plan (parallel with objective prep)

**Weeks 1–4 — Theme building phase**

- 2 themes per week (~30 min per theme to draft).
- No writing yet. Just building the content base.
- End of Week 4: ~8 themes complete.

**Weeks 5–8 — Answer structure phase**

- 2 themes per week (new) + write 1 full answer per week using previously-built themes.
- Answer format: 500-word response to a past-paper-style question. Me review structure, depth, data usage.

**Weeks 9–10 — Integration phase**

- Write 2 answers per week across mixed themes.
- Cross-link themes (inflation answer pulls from monetary policy + fiscal policy + external sector).

**Weeks 11–12 — Timed drills**

- Full Phase 2 paper simulation (3 questions × 500 words in 90 min).
- Handwriting speed check.

### English descriptive (RBI Paper 2, SEBI)

Runs alongside Track B, but different skill:

- Essay (current affairs, economy, social) — same themes as above.
- Precis (summary of a given passage to ~1/3 length).
- Reading comprehension descriptive — short answers from a passage.

Prep: 1 precis + 1 comprehension per week starting Week 5.

## AI workflow

Same pattern as quant:

**Building a theme:**

```text
Build theme: <name>. Use the theme-note format from descriptive-starter-plan.md.
```

Me draft a 1-page note. You edit and commit.

**Writing an answer:**

```text
Write answer on: <prompt>. I'll use themes <X, Y, Z>. Review my draft for structure, depth, data.
```

You write, me review and score.

**Logging:**

```text
Log answer: <date> | <prompt> | <score/feedback summary>.
```

Goes into `notes/descriptive-writing/answer-log.md`.
