# English Language — Starter Plan (Pareto 80/20)

The 20% of English that decides ~80% of marks across SBI / IBPS / RBI / NABARD / SEBI / LIC.

## The core insight

English is **not a study subject — it is a skill.** Reading the right things daily beats solving 500 grammar MCQs. Reading Comprehension (RC) alone carries 30–50% of the English section in every exam.

**Translation:** daily reading > chapter-wise drills.

## The Pareto Map

### Tier 1 — High-yield core (start here)

1. **Reading Comprehension (RC)** — 30–50% of marks. Every exam. Non-negotiable daily.
2. **Cloze Test** — 5–10 marks. Vocabulary + grammar + context. Improves with RC.
3. **Grammar basics** — Subject-verb agreement, tenses, prepositions, articles, pronouns. The 5 topics that produce 90% of Error Spotting / Sentence Improvement questions.
4. **Error Spotting / Sentence Improvement** — direct grammar application. Builds on Tier 1.3.
5. **Para Jumbles** — logic + connector awareness. 3–5 marks.

### Tier 2 — Medium-yield (after Tier 1 fluent)

- Fill in the Blanks (single + double)
- Sentence Rearrangement (different from para jumbles)
- Word Usage / Vocab-in-context
- Sentence Completion / Connectors

### Tier 3 — Low-yield / exam-specific

- Spotting the antonym/synonym in a passage (new pattern RBI/SBI)
- Word Swap / Word Rearrangement (new pattern)
- Idioms & Phrases (minimal direct questions these days)

## Why this order

RC + Cloze + Grammar basics = a **single compounding skill** (reading with comprehension). Everything else in English is a sub-case or variant. Drill these three in parallel and the rest of the section starts solving itself.

## The Daily Minimum (non-negotiable)

This is the habit that carries English. Do this every single day, even on low-energy days:

- **1 editorial** from The Hindu, Indian Express, or Livemint (~800 words, 15 min).
- **1 RC passage** (exam-style, ~500 words + 5–10 questions, 15 min).
- **10 words** added to your vocab note — only words encountered in the editorial or RC, never random lists.

**Total:** ~35 min/day. Skip this and English scores flatten.

## Start Here: Reading Comprehension (Week 1)

**Why RC first:** It's the highest-weight sub-section, the hardest to cram, and the easiest to lose marks to if you read slowly.

### What to actually learn

- **Question-type recognition** — every RC question is one of 6 types:
  - Main idea / central theme
  - Factual (directly stated in passage)
  - Inference (implied, not stated)
  - Vocabulary-in-context
  - Tone / author's attitude
  - Structural (purpose of paragraph X, etc.)
- **Reading strategy** — skim first (60 sec), then read questions, then re-read relevant sections. Do NOT read the passage fully upfront.
- **Elimination over selection** — in RC, 2 options are always wrong; narrow to 2, then decide.
- **Trap options** — "extreme language" (always, never, completely), "out of scope", "opposite sentiment".

### Common traps

- Selecting an option that is *true in real life* but not supported by the passage.
- Missing the word "not" in the question stem.
- Inference questions answered with factual options (too literal).
- Tone questions answered with emotional rather than analytical options.

## The AI-Assisted Study Loop (per sub-topic)

Similar to quant, but English runs in **parallel tracks** (daily habit + weekly focus topic).

### Daily track (every day)

See "Daily Minimum" above. 35 min.

### Weekly focus track (Week 1 = RC, Week 2 = Cloze, etc.)

**Day 1 — Concept + patterns (60 min)**

- Ask Claude: *"Explain [sub-topic]. What are the question-type patterns? What are the 3–5 common traps?"*
- Write a 1-page note in [notes/english/](../notes/english/) — concept map + traps.

**Day 2–3 — Untimed practice (45 min/day)**

- 5 questions per day, untimed. Analyze each wrong answer.
- Paste tricky ones to Claude: *"Why is option B wrong? What was the trap?"*

**Day 4 — Timed practice (45 min)**

- 10 questions, strict time.
- Log errors in [mistake-log](../notes/english/mistake-log.md) if created.

**Day 5 — Review + integration**

- Re-read the weekly note. Add any traps discovered during practice.
- One mixed set (RC + Cloze + Grammar) for integration.

## Weekly Rhythm

- **Mon–Fri:** 30 min daily minimum + 30 min on weekly focus topic = 60 min/day.
- **Sat:** 45-min sectional (30 English questions, 20 min) + analysis.
- **Sun:** Editorial + vocab review. Light day.

## Pareto Checkpoints

- **End of Week 2:** RC + Cloze fluent. 70%+ accuracy untimed.
- **End of Week 4:** Grammar basics covered. Error Spotting at 70%+.
- **End of Week 6:** Para Jumbles + full Tier 1. First sectional mock.
- **End of Week 8:** Tier 2 covered. Sectional speed = target exam pace.

## Source Discipline

**One editorial source:** pick ONE — The Hindu OR Indian Express OR Livemint. Rotate causes confusion. Hindu is standard for RBI-style; Livemint is sharper for finance angle.

**One RC practice source:** any one book (Wren & Martin for grammar; Plinth to Paramount for comprehensive; Adda247 / Oliveboard PDFs for exam-style).

**One vocab source:** the editorial itself. No random vocab apps. No separate word lists.

## The Reading ↔ Descriptive loop

Daily editorial reading isn't just for RC. Every editorial gives you:

- Opening phrases for descriptive essays.
- Data points and schemes to quote.
- Counter-arguments for balance.

Log 1–2 useful lines per editorial into [notes/descriptive-writing/track-a-po/reusable-paras.md](../notes/descriptive-writing/track-a-po/reusable-paras.md) or theme files.

**One action, three skills grown:** English RC, GA/current affairs, descriptive writing.

## What NOT to do

- Don't start with grammar rules. Grammar without reading is inert. Build reading first, use grammar to fix errors you encounter.
- Don't memorize vocabulary lists. You forget words out of context. Learn words *from* your reading.
- Don't drill rare topics (synonyms/antonyms in isolation, idioms lists) before Tier 1 is at 70%+ accuracy.
- Don't read fiction for exam prep. Editorials train the right register.
