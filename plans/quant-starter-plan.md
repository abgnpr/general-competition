# Quantitative Aptitude — Starter Plan (Pareto 80/20)

The 20% of quant that decides ~80% of marks across SBI / IBPS / RBI / NABARD / SEBI / LIC.

## The Pareto Map

### Tier 1 — High-yield core (start here)

These chapters appear in every exam, feed DI, and compound into everything else. Nail these first.

1. **Percentages** — the master key. Unlocks P&L, SI/CI, DI, Ratios.
2. **Ratio & Proportion** — feeds Partnership, Ages, Mixtures, DI.
3. **Averages** — feeds DI, Ages, mixtures.
4. **Profit & Loss** — pure percentage application.
5. **Simple & Compound Interest** — pure percentage application.
6. **Time & Work** (+ Pipes & Cisterns) — ratio/LCM thinking.
7. **Time, Speed & Distance** (+ Trains, Boats & Streams) — ratio + linear equations.
8. **Data Interpretation** — Tables, Bar, Line, Pie, Caselets. Built on the chapters above.
9. **Simplification & Approximation** — daily speed-math warmup.
10. **Number Series** — pattern recognition, low-cost marks.

### Tier 2 — Medium-yield (after Tier 1)

- Mixtures & Alligation, Partnership, Ages, Boats & Streams, Trains
- Quadratic Equations (PO exams)
- Mensuration (Area, Volume — limited sub-topics)
- Permutation & Combination + Probability (basic only)

### Tier 3 — Low-yield / postpone

Logarithms, Surds & Indices, Stocks & Shares, True Discount, Banker's Discount, Height & Distance, Races, Calendar, Clocks. Touch only if time permits.

## Why this order

Percentages + Ratios + Averages are **load-bearing**. Every arithmetic chapter and every DI set is a disguised version of these three. Master them and later chapters take a fraction of the effort.

## Start Here: Percentages (Week 1)

**Why Percentages first:** Highest compounding leverage. P&L, SI/CI, DI, and half of reasoning-math are percentage problems in costume.

### What to actually learn

- Conversion fluency: fractions ↔ decimals ↔ percentages (memorize 1/2 through 1/20 by heart).
- Percentage change (increase/decrease), successive percentage change.
- Percentage → multiplication factor trick (e.g., 20% increase = ×1.2).
- Base change problems ("A is 25% more than B → B is what % less than A").
- Word-problem translation: income/expenditure, population, exam marks.

### Common traps

- Mixing up "% of" vs "% more than" vs "% less than."
- Forgetting the base shifts after each step in successive changes.
- Applying additive logic where multiplicative is needed.

## The AI-Assisted Study Loop (per chapter)

Use this loop with Claude (or any LLM) for every Tier 1 chapter. Roughly 5–7 days per chapter for Tier 1.

### Day 1 — Concept map (60–90 min)

- Ask Claude: *"Explain [chapter] from zero — core idea, the 3–5 formulas that actually matter, and the mental model for attacking problems in under 45 seconds."*
- Write the concept map in your own words in `notes/quantitative-aptitude/<chapter>.md` (front matter + 1 page max).
- Do not start problems yet.

### Day 2 — Worked examples (90 min)

- Pick 10 problems of increasing difficulty from RS Aggarwal / Arun Sharma / any one source.
- Solve untimed. For each, after solving, ask Claude: *"Is there a faster approach? What's the trap in this problem?"*
- Log the faster approach, not the long one.

### Day 3 — Speed drill (60 min)

- 20 problems, timed (45 sec each). Pen-paper only.
- After: feed wrong/slow problems to Claude → *"Why did I get this wrong/slow? What pattern should I have recognized?"*

### Day 4 — Mixed recall (45 min)

- Ask Claude to quiz you with 10 mixed problems from the chapter (varied difficulty). No solutions until you attempt.
- Focus: pattern recognition, not novelty.

### Day 5 — Mistake log + trap sheet (30 min)

- Add a "Common Traps" section to your chapter note. Max 5 bullets.
- Add 3–5 shortcuts you discovered in `notes/quantitative-aptitude/<chapter>-shortcuts.md`.

### Day 6–7 — DI integration

- Solve 3–5 DI sets that lean on this chapter (e.g., after Percentages, solve Pie Charts).
- The test of mastery: can you compute percentages mentally inside DI, without a detour?

## Weekly Rhythm (working professional friendly)

- **Mon–Fri:** 60–90 min/day on current chapter (one of the loop steps above).
- **Sat:** 2-hour mixed session — previous chapter revision + DI set.
- **Sun:** Sectional test (30 quant questions, 20 min) + analysis. Analysis > attempt.

## Pareto Checkpoints

- **End of Week 4:** Tier 1 chapters 1–5 done (Percentages, Ratios, Averages, P&L, SI/CI). You should solve any of these problems in under 45s.
- **End of Week 8:** Tier 1 complete + DI basics. First full sectional mock.
- **End of Week 12:** Tier 2 covered. Sectional speed = target exam cutoff pace.

If you're behind schedule, **cut Tier 2/3, never Tier 1**. The 80/20 is only useful if you respect the 20.

## What NOT to do

- Don't start with Number System, HCF/LCM, Decimal Fractions. Low exam yield for the page count.
- Don't chase every shortcut YouTube video. Learn the concept, then adopt shortcuts that *you* found useful during practice.
- Don't buy a second book before finishing one. One source, multiple revisions.
- Don't time yourself in Week 1 of a chapter. Speed is a side effect of clarity, not a substitute.
