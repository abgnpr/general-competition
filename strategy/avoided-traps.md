# Avoided Traps

Common pitfalls in banking exam preparation and how to steer clear of them.

## Resource Hoarding

Collecting too many books, PDFs, apps, and Telegram groups — finishing none. **Rule:** One subject, one book, one CA source. Anything extra must replace, not add.

## Delaying Mocks

Skipping mocks until "I finish the syllabus." Start mocks early, even if scores are ugly. They're diagnostics, not verdicts.

## Ignoring Subjects

Postponing English or GA till "later." Both need daily contact — even 30 minutes counts.

## Studying Without a Timer

Banking exams reward speed + accuracy. Practice under timed conditions from Month 2 onwards.

## Skipping Revision

Forgetting what you studied 2 weeks ago. Multiple revisions of one source > one read of many sources.

## Not Analyzing Mocks

Taking mocks without spending equal or more time analyzing mistakes. Analysis is sacred time, not optional homework.

## Overloading After a Break

Missed a day? Never compensate by overloading the next day. Just resume normally.

## Comparison Trap

Comparing your Day 10 with someone else's Year 2. Track your own improvement only.

## Multiple CA Sources

Following multiple apps, multiple PDFs, Telegram overload. Pick one daily + one monthly source and stick.

## UPSC-Style Over-Preparation

Using UPSC yearly compilations for banking. They're too bulky and padded with irrelevant static content. Stick to monthly PDFs covering last 6-8 months.

## Bulky Daily Notes

Making detailed daily notes from newspaper reading. Use micro-notes instead: 1-2 lines per fact, only if it can become an MCQ or analytical question.
