# Common Subject Base (70-80% Shared Across Exams)

The bulk of banking exam preparation overlaps. Master this base and you're competitive in most exams.

## Quantitative Aptitude

**Common prep (all exams):**

- Arithmetic is non-negotiable: Percentages, Ratio & Proportion, Averages, Time & Work, SI/CI, Profit & Loss, Time Speed Distance
- DI fundamentals: Tables, Bar graphs, Line graphs, Pie charts, Caselets
- Number Series, Quadratic Equations, Simplification, Approximation

**Extra for PO exams:**

- Timed sets, mental math and shortcuts

**Extra for RBI:**

- Concept clarity over tricks

## Reasoning Ability

**Common prep (all exams):**

- Puzzles, Seating Arrangement (linear, circular, floor-based)
- Syllogism (new pattern), Inequality, Blood Relations, Direction Sense
- Coding-Decoding, Order & Ranking, Input-Output, Data Sufficiency

**PO focus:**

- High-speed puzzle solving, new pattern exposure

**RBI focus:**

- Logical reasoning, not marathon puzzles

## English Language

**Common prep (all exams):**

- Reading Comprehension — every single day
- Grammar basics: subject-verb agreement, tenses, prepositions
- Error Spotting, Cloze Test, Sentence Improvement, Para Jumbles, Fill in the Blanks

**RBI advantage:**

- Editorial reading (The Hindu / Indian Express) improves both RCs and descriptive writing

## General Awareness

- Current Affairs (last 6 months) — banking, economy, policy
- Banking Awareness: RBI policies, banking terms, recent banking news
- Static GK: HQs, taglines, capitals, currencies, important days
- Financial Awareness, Government Schemes

**Key difference:** For SBI/IBPS, focus on facts. For RBI, focus on reasons + implications.

## Computer Knowledge

- Basics of Computer, Networking, Internet, MS Office, Database, Security
- Mostly factual, easy marks — postpone to later phase
