# Current Affairs Strategy

The spine of all three exams. Prepare once, use thrice.

## Core Rule

Banking exams need **factual recall** — unlike UPSC, you cannot afford vague memory. Every fact matters for MCQs.

- For SBI/IBPS: focus on **facts**
- For RBI: focus on **reasons + implications**

## Daily Sources (pick ONE newspaper, ONE portal)

### Newspapers

- **The Hindu** — National and Business sections (great for RC + conceptual understanding)
- **Indian Express** — Economy & Policy (deeper analysis)
- **Mint / Livemint** — Banking & finance focus

### Economic Focus

- **RBI Website** — Press releases & bulletins (official source for monetary policy, repo rate)
- **Press Information Bureau (PIB)** — Government facts and policy pronouncements

### Exam-Focused Portals (pick ONE)

- VisionIAS Current Affairs
- InsightsIAS Daily Current Affairs
- Drishti IAS Current Affairs Daily
- AffairsCloud (banking-friendly, daily + monthly)

### Monthly Compilations (pick ONE)

- VisionIAS Monthly CA PDF
- InsightsIAS Monthly CA PDF
- AffairsCloud Monthly

### YouTube (bite-sized, 5-10 min clips)

- Study IQ — Economy & Banking playlists
- Wifistudy — Current Affairs / Banking
- Adda247 — Banking/Finance videos

### Apps (install ONE, no more)

- Inshorts — quick 2-4 line headlines for daily scanning
- The Hindu App — longer coverage, good for RC + depth
- PIB App — government notifications

## What to Read (High-Yield Focus Areas)

**Banking & Money Markets:** RBI policy news, rate changes (CRR, SLR), banking reforms, NPAs, credit growth, financial inclusion

**Economy & Policy:** GDP growth data, inflation reports, budget announcements, fiscal deficit, World Bank/IMF reports

**Government Announcements:** Schemes, reforms, Parliament session outcomes

**Global Economy (bite-size):** Fed rate changes, oil price trends, FX markets, World Bank/IMF forecasts

**Ignore:** Political drama, crime, courts, local issues

## Daily Workflow (40-45 min total)

**Morning (30 min):**

- Newspaper reading — economy + banking sections only
- Read slowly, no panic

**Night (10-15 min):**

- Write micro-notes (4-5 bullet points max):
  - What happened
  - Why it matters (1 line)
- Only facts/opinions with exam value
- No bulky notes, no copying articles

**Micro-note rule:** If a fact can become a one-liner MCQ or a "Why did RBI do X?" question, write 1-2 lines. Otherwise skip.

## Weekly Workflow (Sunday, 60-90 min)

- Revise last 7 days' CA micro-notes
- Fill gaps using monthly CA PDF (selectively)
- Attempt 20-30 GA MCQs (banking focus)

## Monthly Workflow (one fixed weekend slot, 3-4 hours)

1. **Read 1** — Monthly CA PDF, relaxed read, understand themes
2. **Read 2** — Exam lens: Facts (SBI/IBPS), Reasons/impact (RBI)
3. **Condense** — 4-5 pages max (or mental condensation)

One monthly source only. No source-hopping.

## Revision Cycle

- **Weekly:** Revise last 7 days (30 min)
- **Monthly:** Re-read monthly PDF twice
- **Before exam:** Last 3-4 months get 3-4 revisions; older months get 1-2 quick scans
- Depth of last 6-8 months > breadth of 12 months

## Avoid

- Multiple apps, multiple PDFs, Telegram overload
- UPSC-style yearly compilations (too bulky for banking, too much static padding)
- Daily bulky note-making (wastes time, creates "notes for notes' sake")
