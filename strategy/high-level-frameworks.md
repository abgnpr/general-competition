# High-Level Frameworks

Mental models and decision frameworks for exam-day and preparation.

## The Two Modes

All preparation operates in two modes:

- **Analysis mode** — deep understanding, conceptual clarity (RBI-oriented)
- **Turbo mode** — speed, pattern recognition, quick decisions (PO-oriented)

Train both. Deploy based on which exam is next.

## Exam-Day Strategy

1. Scan the paper quickly before starting
2. Attempt your strongest section first to build confidence
3. Skip questions that take too long — come back if time allows
4. Never leave easy marks on the table chasing tough questions
5. Watch the clock: allocate time per section and stick to it

## Mock Test Analysis Framework

### Dual Analysis Method

After every mock, analyze from two angles:

**Speed analysis (PO lens):**

- Which questions took too long?
- Where can I apply shortcuts next time?
- What's my attempt rate vs accuracy rate?

**Concept analysis (RBI lens):**

- What concept was I unclear about?
- What needs deeper study?
- Can I explain *why* the answer is correct?

## Error Classification

Categorize every mistake into one of four types:

| Type | Meaning | Fix |
|------|---------|-----|
| **Conceptual** | Didn't understand the topic | Go back to basics, re-study |
| **Silly** | Knew the concept, made a careless error | Slow down, double-check |
| **Time** | Ran out of time, couldn't attempt | Speed drills, better question selection |
| **Didn't attempt** | Skipped due to low confidence | Practice more of that topic type |

Track patterns over multiple mocks. If one error type dominates, that's where to focus.

## Topic Prioritization Framework

Prioritize topics by ROI (marks per hour invested):

1. **High frequency + your strength** — drill these for speed (quick wins)
2. **High frequency + your weakness** — invest study time here (highest ROI)
3. **Low frequency + your strength** — maintain, don't over-invest
4. **Low frequency + your weakness** — skip or do minimal (time sink)
