# RBI Grade B — Core Layer

What RBI Grade B adds on top of the common subject base. This is what most PO-only aspirants lack — and your edge.

## Phase I (Prelims) — Additional

- General Awareness section is part of Prelims (unlike IBPS/SBI where GA is Mains-only)
- Higher difficulty level in Quant and Reasoning
- Speed matters less, conceptual clarity matters more

## Phase II (Mains)

### Paper I — Economic & Social Issues (ESI)

- Growth & Development, Indian Economy, Globalisation
- Social Structure, Poverty & Unemployment, Sustainable Development
- Social Sector Initiatives, Financial Inclusion
- Demographic Trends, Education & Health
- **Approach:** Link theory to current events — inflation, GDP, monetary policy

### Paper II — English (Descriptive)

- Essay, Precis, Comprehension
- Higher standard than PO descriptive
- Editorial reading builds this skill naturally

### Paper III — Finance & Management

**Finance:** Financial System, Financial Markets, Banking, Insurance, Government Securities, Public Finance, International Finance

**Management:** Planning, Organizing, Staffing, Directing, Controlling, Motivation, Leadership, Communication, HR Management — thinkers only at concept level

## Key Resources

- Indian Economy — Ramesh Singh (selected chapters only)
- Finance & Management — RBI Grade B guide (any one standard edition)
- RBI website reports (press releases, bulletins)
- Budget & Economic Survey summaries

## RBI-Specific Study Approach

- Start economics early (Month 1: inflation, GDP, repo rate, CPI vs WPI)
- Banking system and financial markets from Month 2
- Finance & Management concepts from Month 3
- Begin descriptive answer structure from Month 3
