# Working Professional Timetable

## Constraints

- Full-time job (~9-6)
- Can squeeze small pockets at work
- Serious study happens morning + night
- Target: ~3.5-4 hours on weekdays, more on weekends

## Weekday Schedule

| Time Slot | Duration | Activity |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| Morning (before office) | 45 min | Core subject (rotational — see weekly-monthly-structure.md) |
| Morning | 30 min | Newspaper reading (TH or IE — economy + banking only) |
| Morning | 15 min | Micro-notes (4-5 bullet points max) |
| During work (breaks) | 20-30 min | Revise yesterday's CA micro-notes OR 5-10 GA MCQs OR revise one economics concept |
| Night (after office) | 60-75 min | Practice — Quant/Reasoning questions (timed sets once comfortable) |
| Night | 30 min | English (RC practice) OR RBI core (Economics/Finance concept reading) |
| Night | 15 min | Quick CA glance — skim today's news or monthly PDF |

**Total weekday: ~3.5-4 hours**

## Weekend Schedule

| Day | Duration | Activity |
|-----|----------|----------|
| Saturday | 2-3 hrs | Core subjects + backlog |
| Saturday | 1 hr | RBI Economics/Finance |
| Sunday | 2-3 hrs | 1 full mock OR 2 sectional tests + deep analysis |
| Sunday | 1-1.5 hrs | Weekly CA revision |

## Non-Negotiables (every day, even on bad days)

- 30 min newspaper reading
- At minimum, read the newspaper. Low energy day? That still counts.

## At-Work Rules

- No new topics at work. Only revision or light intake.
- Use break time for: revising micro-notes, editorial re-reads, GA MCQs on phone
